How to Use Adobe Illustrator's "Gradient" Tool For Your Journalism 108 Class

Time Required: 40 minutes
Helpfulness rating: 100%

Are you having troubles navigating Adobe Illustrator when finishing your Journalism 108 challenge? Well, look no further than this Wiki How, which will teach you how to find, use, and master one of the many useful tools of Adobe Illustrator: the gradient tool!

  • Things You'll Need

  • A computer with access to Adobe Illustrator
  • An Adobe Account (to save your work)
  • Time

  1. Step 1 - Open Adobe Illustrator and click "Create New" on the left hand side:

    You wil always need to create a new document on Adobe Illustrator. In Journalism 108, you will be working with multiple dimensions for different projects.



  2. Step 2 - Create the outline of your document:

    Since we use twitter in our Journalism 180 Class, let's create a new document in P.P.I. (Pixels Per Inch). When using Adobe for Twitter, always set your width to 1200 and your height to 675 P.P.I.

    You will also set the Color Mode to R.G.B., and the Raster Effects to 300 PPI.

  3. Step 3 - Create a regular rectangle:

    Make sure this rectangle covers the entire dimension of your document. Its color should either be black or white.

  4. Step 4 - Click on the gradient tool on the bottom left in Adobe Illustrator:

    Once selected, click on the center of your rectangle, and a gradient should appear.

  5. Step 5 - Customize your gradient:

    Now that yu have the gradient, you can change up the way it looks, what color it is, and how transparent it can be. Watch the video below for more practice and understanding.
